Driving Licence Changes

With effect from the 8th June the DVLA will be abolishing paper counterpart driving licences for GB licences (so ex Northern Ireland). This will in turn affect the procedures that we adopt with regard to licence checking.

Clients will be able to access their own counterpart by visiting the following website https://www.viewdrivingrecord.service.gov.uk/driving-record/licence-number They will be required to input their driving licence number, postcode and National Insurance number.

In all cases where you currently request and validate a copy driving licence, you must now request a copy of the photocard and ask them to download a copy of their counterpart which will be in PDF format, and they can either email or send this to us for checking. The counterpart is dated with the time that the document is produced and we would need to see a current download and not an old one. The client has an option to request code that they can forward us to check the licence. However as this code is only valid for 72 hours I do not recommend us using this process unless we are dealing with the matter in real time over the telephone. In these circumstances we need the last 8 digits of their driving licence number and the code and need to visit https://www.viewdrivingrecord.service.gov.uk/driving-record/validate With regard to Companies and fleet policies, Employers do have the option to purchase checking services which carry out checks without the requirement for the above.
